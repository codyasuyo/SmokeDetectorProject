Nuclear Physics
Americium-241
Alpha Decay
Every smoke detector holds a sealed Am-241 source. It decays by emitting alpha particles — helium-4 nuclei — ~37,000 times per second.

Chemistry
Air Ionization
in the Chamber
Alpha particles knock electrons from N₂ and O₂ molecules, creating positive ions and free electrons — a tiny, steady ion current between two charged plates.
N₂ + α → N₂⁺ + e⁻ O₂ + α → O₂⁺ + e⁻ I ≈ 10⁻¹² A (picoamps)

Mechanism
Smoke Kills
the Ion Current
Smoke particles are large combustion byproducts — carbon soot and aerosols. They attach to ions in the chamber, neutralizing them so they can't reach the plates.
Smoke + O₂⁺ → [particle·O₂]⁺ → ion immobilized → current drops
🚨
Electronics
Current Drop →
Alarm Sounds
A comparator circuit monitors the picoamp current 24/7. When smoke drops it below a threshold, the circuit fires the piezoelectric buzzer at ~85 dB.
Normal: current flows Smoke: current drops → comparator fires → ~85 dB buzzer!
⚛️
Why Alpha Particles?
Alpha particles (2p+2n) are large and highly ionizing — they create dense ion pairs in a short path. Beta or gamma emitters would pass straight through without ionizing the air efficiently. Alphas are also stopped by the metal housing, making the source completely safe for home use.
🧪
Dual-Chamber Design
Real detectors have two chambers: one open to air (sensing) and one sealed (reference). The circuit compares both. This cancels humidity, pressure, and aging effects — only a relative change in the open chamber from smoke triggers the alarm.
Sensing ÷ Reference = ratio Smoke → ratio drops → ALARM
🌫️
Ionization vs. Photoelectric
Ionization detectors excel at fast flaming fires with small smoke particles. Photoelectric detectors shine an LED into a chamber — smoke scatters light onto a sensor — and better detect slow smoldering fires. Modern units often combine both technologies.
🔋
Safety & Regulations
The Am-241 source (~1 µCi) is triple-sealed in ceramic and metal. It poses no radiation hazard in normal use. Detectors require UL 217 listing and NRC licensing in the US. The 9V battery powering the circuit is far more energetic than the radiation source itself.
